Assay Overview

Purpose

The Seahorse XF Cell Mito Stress Test measures key parameters of mitochondrial function by directly measuring the oxygen consumption rate (OCR) of live cells. This assay provides comprehensive information about:

  • Basal respiration
  • ATP production
  • Proton leak
  • Maximal respiration
  • Spare respiratory capacity
  • Non-mitochondrial respiration

Assay Principle

The assay uses sequential injection of mitochondrial inhibitors and uncouplers to reveal different aspects of mitochondrial function:

Injection 1 - Oligomycin: Inhibits ATP synthase (Complex V), revealing ATP-linked respiration

Injection 2 - FCCP: Uncoupler that collapses proton gradient, revealing maximal respiration

Injection 3 - Rotenone/Antimycin A: Complex I & III inhibitors, revealing non-mitochondrial respiration

Using multichannel pipette, remove all but 20 ¬µL of growth medium from each well.

  • 2. Slide pipette tips gently down well wall to bottom

Key Parameters Calculated

Basal Respiration

Baseline oxygen consumption before any compound injection. Reflects cellular ATP demand under baseline conditions.

ATP Production

Decrease in OCR after oligomycin injection. Represents respiration dedicated to ATP synthesis.

Proton Leak

OCR remaining after oligomycin that is not coupled to ATP production. Can indicate mitochondrial damage.

Maximal Respiration

Maximum OCR achieved after FCCP injection. Shows maximum capacity of electron transport chain.

Spare Respiratory Capacity

Difference between maximal and basal respiration. Indicates ability to respond to increased energy demand.

Non-mitochondrial Respiration

OCR remaining after rotenone/antimycin A. Represents oxygen consumption by non-mitochondrial enzymes.

Data Quality Checks

Review these quality metrics:

  • ‚Ä¢ Background wells: Should show minimal, consistent OCR
  • ‚Ä¢ Coefficient of variation (CV): Should be <15% for replicates
  • ‚Ä¢ FCCP response: Should show clear increase in OCR
  • ‚Ä¢ Rotenone/AA response: Should reduce OCR to near baseline
  • ‚Ä¢ Edge effects: Check if edge wells differ significantly from center wells

Normalization

To account for differences in cell number between wells, normalize OCR data to:

  • ‚Ä¢ Total protein content (BCA or Bradford assay)
  • ‚Ä¢ DNA content (PicoGreen or similar)
  • ‚Ä¢ Cell count (automated counting or manual)
  • ‚Ä¢ Imaging-based cell quantification (e.g., BioTek Cytation)

Troubleshooting

Problem: High variability between replicates (CV >20%)

Possible Causes & Solutions:

  • ‚Ä¢
    Uneven cell seeding: Ensure cells are thoroughly mixed before and during seeding. Remix cell suspension every 8-12 wells.
  • ‚Ä¢
    Cell damage during washing: Use gentle technique, never vacuum aspirator. Leave 20 ¬µL minimum between washes.
  • ‚Ä¢
    Edge effects: Ensure 30-min room temp rest after seeding. Use properly humidified incubator.

Problem: No response to FCCP

Possible Causes & Solutions:

  • ‚Ä¢
    FCCP concentration too low: Perform FCCP titration (0.125-2.0 ¬µM) to find optimal concentration for your cell type.
  • ‚Ä¢
    FCCP concentration too high: Excessive FCCP can inhibit respiration. Try lower concentration.
  • ‚Ä¢
    Degraded FCCP: Prepare fresh working solution. Do not reuse reconstituted compounds.

Problem: High background OCR in empty wells

Possible Causes & Solutions:

  • ‚Ä¢
    Cells in background wells: Verify background wells (A1, A12, H1, H12) contain no cells under microscope.
  • ‚Ä¢
    Contaminated medium: Use fresh, sterile assay medium. Filter if necessary.

Problem: Low or no basal respiration

Possible Causes & Solutions:

  • ‚Ä¢
    Too few cells: Increase seeding density. Optimal range is typically 10,000-20,000 cells/well.
  • ‚Ä¢
    Unhealthy cells: Check cell viability and morphology before seeding. Use cells at optimal passage number.
  • ‚Ä¢
    Insufficient substrate: Verify assay medium contains glucose, glutamine, and pyruvate at recommended concentrations.

Problem: Incomplete inhibition by rotenone/antimycin A

Possible Causes & Solutions:

  • ‚Ä¢
    High non-mitochondrial respiration: This is normal for some cell types. Remaining OCR represents non-mitochondrial oxygen consumption.
  • ‚Ä¢
    Insufficient compound delivery: Verify ports loaded correctly and compounds diluted properly.

Problem: Cells detached during washing

Possible Causes & Solutions:

  • ‚Ä¢
    Aggressive pipetting: Dispense media gently along well wall, never directly onto cells.
  • ‚Ä¢
    Weakly adherent cells: Use "gentle washing" method with smaller volumes (see protocol for details).
  • ‚Ä¢
    Cells not fully adhered: Allow longer incubation time after seeding (up to 48 hours for some cell types).
