Overview

This protocol describes lentiviral production using HEK293T packaging cells and subsequent infection of target cells. Lentiviral vectors can transduce both dividing and non-dividing cells, making them ideal for stable gene transfer in a wide range of cell types.

Workflow Overview:

  • ‚Ä¢ Day 0: Plate 293T cells
  • ‚Ä¢ Day 1: Transfection
  • ‚Ä¢ Day 2: Media change
  • ‚Ä¢ Day 3-4: Virus collection
  • ‚Ä¢ Day 4-5: Virus concentration (optional)
  • ‚Ä¢ Day 5+: Target cell infection
  • ‚Ä¢ Day 7-8: Selection or sorting

Applications

  • ‚Ä¢ Stable gene overexpression
  • ‚Ä¢ shRNA/CRISPR knockdown
  • ‚Ä¢ Reporter gene expression
  • ‚Ä¢ Gene editing delivery
  • ‚Ä¢ Cell line engineering
  • ‚Ä¢ Primary cell transduction

Key Components

  • ‚Ä¢ Transfer plasmid: Contains your gene of interest
  • ‚Ä¢ Packaging plasmid: pSPAX2 or cDNL (gag-pol)
  • ‚Ä¢ Envelope plasmid: pMD2.G or VSVG
  • ‚Ä¢ 293T cells: Virus-producing cells
  • ‚Ä¢ Transfection reagent: Delivers DNA to cells

Biosafety Considerations

  • ‚Ä¢ Lentiviral work typically requires BSL-2 containment
  • ‚Ä¢ Check institutional biosafety guidelines and obtain IBC approval
  • ‚Ä¢ VSV-G pseudotyped lentivirus has broad tropism - handle carefully
  • ‚Ä¢ Work in biosafety cabinet for all virus handling steps
  • ‚Ä¢ Decontaminate all materials with 10% bleach before disposal
  • ‚Ä¢ Never pipette by mouth - use mechanical pipettors
  • ‚Ä¢ Wear appropriate PPE: lab coat, gloves, eye protection

Select Transfection Method:

Virus Production - JetPRIME Method

Scale options: 10cm plate (standard) or 6-well/12-well (small scale)

Day 0: Plate 293T Cells

  • Poly-L-lysine coating: Add 5 mL poly-L-lysine to 10cm dish, incubate 30 min at 37¬∞C
  • Aspirate poly-L-lysine (can be reused 2-3 times)
  • 10cm plate: Plate 3-8 √ó 10‚Å∂ 293T cells in 10 mL DMEM + 10% FBS
  • 6-well plate: Plate 150,000 cells/well in 1 mL medium
  • 12-well plate: Plate 75,000 cells/well in 500 ¬µL medium
  • Target: 60-70% confluent on Day 1 (transfection day)
  • Incubate overnight at 37¬∞C, 5% CO‚ÇÇ

Day 1: Transfection

  • Change medium to fresh DMEM + 10% FBS (no antibiotics)
  • Prepare transfection mix per plate:

10cm Plate Recipe:

  • ‚Ä¢ 500 ¬µL JetPRIME buffer
  • ‚Ä¢ 20 ¬µg transfer plasmid (your gene of interest)
  • ‚Ä¢ 15 ¬µg cDNL (packaging plasmid)
  • ‚Ä¢ 2 ¬µg VSVG (envelope plasmid)
  • ‚Ä¢ Vortex, spin down briefly
  • ‚Ä¢ Add 10 ¬µL JetPRIME reagent per plate
  • ‚Ä¢ Vortex, spin down, incubate 10 min at RT

6-well Plate Recipe:

  • ‚Ä¢ 200 ¬µL JetPRIME buffer
  • ‚Ä¢ 2 ¬µg total DNA
  • ‚Ä¢ 4 ¬µL JetPRIME reagent
  • ‚Ä¢ Incubate 10 min at RT
  • Add transfection mix dropwise to cells, distribute evenly
  • Incubate 4-12 hours (no longer than 12h to avoid toxicity)

Day 2: Media Change

  • Aspirate transfection medium
  • Wash once with PBS (optional)
  • Add fresh DMEM + 10% FBS: 15 mL for 10cm plate, 1-2 mL for 6-well
  • Return to incubator

Day 3: First Collection

  • Collect medium into 50 mL conical tube
  • Store at 4¬∞C if collecting multiple batches
  • Add 10-15 mL fresh medium back to plates
  • Return to incubator for ~36 hours

Day 4: Second Collection (Optional)

  • Collect medium and pool with Day 3 collection
  • Total volume: typically 20-30 mL per 10cm plate
  • Discard 293T cells (decontaminate with bleach)

Day 4-5: Clarification

  • Centrifuge pooled virus at 300-500 √ó g for 5 min to pellet cell debris
  • Transfer supernatant to new tube
  • Filter through 0.45 ¬µm syringe filter (work in BSC)
  • Virus is now ready for concentration (optional) or direct use
  • Aliquot and store at -80¬∞C (stable for months)

Virus Production - Generic Protocol

This protocol can be adapted to various transfection reagents. Follow manufacturer's instructions for DNA:reagent ratios.

General Workflow

  1. Day 0: Plate 293T cells at 60-70% confluency for next day
  2. Day 1: Transfect with transfer + packaging + envelope plasmids
  3. Day 2: Change to fresh collection medium
  4. Day 3: First virus collection, replace with fresh medium
  5. Day 4: Second virus collection (optional), pool collections
  6. Day 5: Clarify by centrifugation and filtration

Key Parameters

  • ‚Ä¢ Cell density: 60-70% confluent at transfection
  • ‚Ä¢ Total DNA: 10-20 ¬µg per 10cm plate
  • ‚Ä¢ Plasmid ratio: Transfer : Packaging : Envelope = 4:3:1 to 8:7.5:2.5
  • ‚Ä¢ Collection time: 48-72 hours post-transfection
  • ‚Ä¢ Peak titer: Usually Day 3 collection

Select Concentration Method:

Unconcentrated Virus (Direct Use)

Simplest method - use filtered virus directly

Best for: High-expressing constructs, easily transduced cells, quick experiments

  • After clarification (300 √ó g spin) and 0.45 ¬µm filtration, virus is ready to use
  • Typical volume: 3 mL virus supernatant per 1-2 √ó 10‚Å∂ target cells
  • Add polybrene (8 ¬µg/mL final) to enhance infection
  • Store aliquots at -80¬∞C (avoid freeze-thaw cycles)
  • Titer: Usually 10‚Åµ-10‚Å∂ TU/mL

PEG Precipitation Concentration

Concentrates virus 10-50√ó without special equipment

Best for: Labs without ultracentrifuge, moderate titer needs

Protocol:

  1. Clarify virus: spin 300 √ó g for 5 min, transfer supernatant to new tube
  2. Add PEG-8000 solution at 1:3 ratio (virus:PEG)
  3. Example: 16 mL virus + 6-8 mL PEG solution
  4. Mix by inverting (do not vortex)
  5. Incubate at 4¬∞C for 2 hours (or overnight) with rocking
  6. Centrifuge at 1,500-1,600 √ó g for 1 hour at 4¬∞C
  7. Carefully remove supernatant (virus pellet at bottom)
  8. Resuspend pellet in 1 mL Opti-MEM by pipetting gently
  9. Aliquot 200 ¬µL into cryovials, store at -80¬∞C

Expected concentration: ~20-50√ó original titer

Ultracentrifugation Concentration

Highest concentration (100-1000√ó) and purity

Best for: High-titer needs, difficult-to-transduce cells, in vivo experiments

Protocol:

  1. Pre-clarify virus: centrifuge 300-500 √ó g for 10 min at 4¬∞C
  2. Transfer supernatant to Beckman ultracentrifuge tube
  3. Balance tubes precisely (within 0.1 g)
  4. Ultracentrifuge: 25,000 RPM for 1.5 hours at 4¬∞C
  5. Carefully aspirate supernatant (virus pellet may not be visible)
  6. Resuspend pellet in 200-500 ¬µL Opti-MEM or PBS
  7. Incubate on ice 1-2 hours to fully resuspend
  8. Aliquot and store at -80¬∞C

Ultracentrifuge Settings:

  • ‚Ä¢ Speed: 25,000 RPM (or 50,000-100,000 √ó g)
  • ‚Ä¢ Duration: 1.5 hours
  • ‚Ä¢ Temperature: 4¬∞C
  • ‚Ä¢ Brake: Slow

Target Cell Infection (Transduction)

Spinoculation method enhances infection efficiency

Centrifugation brings virus particles into contact with target cells

Day 0: Prepare Target Cells

  • Ensure target cells are in log-phase growth
  • Viability should be >95%
  • Count cells and prepare appropriate number for infection

Day 1: Infection with Spinoculation

  • Count 1-2 √ó 10‚Å∂ target cells into 15 mL tube
  • Centrifuge and aspirate supernatant
  • For unconcentrated virus: Add 3 mL filtered virus
  • For concentrated virus: Add 20-50 ¬µL virus in 2-3 mL medium
  • Add polybrene to final concentration 8 ¬µg/mL
  • Mix gently
  • Spinoculate: Centrifuge at 3,000 RPM (900 √ó g) for 1.5 hours at 32¬∞C
  • After spin, resuspend cells and transfer to 6-well plate
  • Incubate at 37¬∞C

4 Hours Post-Infection

  • Add 2-3 mL fresh medium (appropriate for your cells - RPMI, DMEM, etc.)
  • Do not remove virus-containing medium
  • Dilutes polybrene and provides fresh nutrients

Day 2: Media Change

  • Centrifuge cells and aspirate virus-containing medium
  • Resuspend in fresh complete medium
  • Return to culture

Day 5-8: Selection or Analysis

  • For fluorescent markers: Sort by FACS on Day 7-8
  • For selection markers: Begin antibiotic selection on Day 3-5
  • Common antibiotics: Puromycin (1-5 ¬µg/mL), Blasticidin (5-10 ¬µg/mL), G418 (400-800 ¬µg/mL)
  • Maintain selection pressure for 5-7 days
  • Untransduced control cells should die within 3-5 days

Alternative: Plate-Based Infection (No Spinoculation)

  • ‚Ä¢ Seed cells in 6-well plate at desired density
  • ‚Ä¢ Add virus + polybrene (8 ¬µg/mL) directly to wells
  • ‚Ä¢ Incubate 24 hours, then change medium
  • ‚Ä¢ Lower efficiency than spinoculation but simpler

Tips for Success

  • Use low-passage 293T cells (<P20)
  • Verify plasmid integrity before transfection
  • Collect virus 48-72h post-transfection (peak titer)
  • Filter virus immediately after collection
  • Aliquot virus to minimize freeze-thaw
  • Optimize MOI for each cell type (typically 0.5-5)
  • Include untransduced controls for selection

Safety Reminders

  • All work in BSL-2 biosafety cabinet
  • Decontaminate with 10% bleach (30 min contact)
  • Autoclave all contaminated materials
  • Never store lentivirus in shared -20¬∞C freezers
  • Label all tubes clearly: "LENTIVIRUS - BSL-2"
  • Report spills to EHS immediately
